The Challenge

Bridging the Gap

Visually impaired individuals often lack real-time awareness of their surroundings. Daily activities such as navigating streets, identifying objects, recognizing people, and detecting signs can become difficult. This creates dependency on external support systems and limits true independence.

The Solution

EchoSight Accessibility

By providing an advanced ML pipeline, EchoSight seamlessly converts physical surroundings into immediate, high-accuracy conversational feedback. It breaks down the barriers of traditional vision dependencies by translating spatial pixels directly into semantic environmental awareness.

Hardware Integration

From AI Model to Wearable Device

The current ML model can be seamlessly integrated into a wearable headset or smart glasses device.

📷

Camera Module

A small camera mounted on the headset captures the user's surroundings in real time. The video feed is sent directly to the AI model for analysis.

🖥

Edge Processing Unit

A compact processor such as a Raspberry Pi, Jetson Nano, or an edge AI processor runs the ML model locally. This allows real-time object detection and face recognition without internet dependency.

🎧

Audio Feedback System

The processed information is converted into audio feedback. Embedded hardware (like bone conduction headphones or small integrated speakers) allows the user to hear detection results while still hearing environmental sounds.

🔋

Power System

A lightweight battery powers the entire system, allowing several hours of continuous, uninterrupted use.

Example Real-World Workflow

A blind user wearing the headset walks through a hallway. The camera captures the surroundings. The AI model processes the video feed and detects objects and people. Audio output might say:

"Chair detected on your left"
"Text ahead"

This gives the user real-time awareness of their surroundings dynamically as they move.

Frequently Asked Questions

EchoSight utilizes a localized neural processing pipeline to intake visual data via a wearable camera, process identifying features through object, facial, and OCR models, and relay that context back to the user via an automated speech synthesis engine.

By constantly parsing the frame for 1000+ classifications of objects and evaluating their relative distance based on bounding box proportions, the system can warn users of immediate hazards (like chairs or cars) within 1 meter of collision.

Text detection isolates printed characters in the environment. Unlike standard readers, our engine evaluates the unicode signatures to identify exact reading languages (English, Hindi, Tamil) on signs, menus, or labels, alerting the user to localized information.

We leverage native and cloud-based TTS engines prioritizing high-fidelity human voices. A specialized queueing architecture prevents audio spam by caching recent identifiers, only Vocalizing new objects or pressing obstacles.
